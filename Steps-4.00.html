Key Insights: Positive


In 2009, there was a notable 1.23% decrease in suicide-related deaths, marking a positive turn in the battle against suicide. The positive trend persisted into 2010, with a further 0.85% decrease, signifying substantial improvement in addressing this critical public health issue. These declines reflect ongoing efforts in suicide prevention and mental health interventions that have proven effective in reducing the incidence of suicide. 


The year 2013 witnessed a commendable 1.81% decrease in suicide-related deaths, marking a continued positive trend in the battle against suicide. This significant decline underscores the ongoing effectiveness of suicide prevention efforts and mental health interventions.


 The period from 2013 to 2015 witnessed a notable and sustained decrease in suicide rates, reflecting positive strides in suicide prevention. In 2013, the suicide rate decreased to 1.86%, and this positive trend continued into 2014 with a further decrease to 1.24%. The remarkable decline persisted in 2015, with a continued decrease to 1.60%. These consecutive decreases highlight the effectiveness of ongoing efforts in suicide prevention, signaling progress in addressing mental health challenges.
