The years spanning from 1991 to 1994 witnessed a concerning trend of increasing suicide-related deaths. In 1991, there was a significant rise with a 2.41% increase, followed by a more substantial increase in 1992, reaching 5.15%. The upward trend persisted in 1993, with a further 4.77% increase, and although a slight decrease was observed in 1994, it remained at 0.12%. These consecutive increases underscore a critical period in which suicide prevention efforts faced challenges.


The year 2014 marked a concerning period in the battle against suicide, witnessing an alarming 2.47% increase in suicide-related deaths. This surge raises critical questions about the factors contributing to this sudden rise and the effectiveness of existing suicide prevention measures during that specific timeframe.
