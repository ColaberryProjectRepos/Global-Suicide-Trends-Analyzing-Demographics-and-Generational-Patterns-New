Executive Summary and Problems Encountered 3

Executive Summary:

This project aims to show how Suicide is a silent killer and it needs a serious attention throughout the world. Despite a total global suicide count of 7 million over the period of 1985-2015, the suicide rate has been observed at 0.38%, revealing a complex interplay between suicide incidents and population dynamics. The year-on-year (YoY) changes further highlight intriguing patterns, with suicide YoY increasing by 2.18% while suicide rate YoY shows a contrasting decrease of -1.60%. This prompts the need for a comprehensive analysis to understand the underlying factors influencing global suicide trends, considering both absolute numbers and rates, in relation to population fluctuations. Such insights can contribute to the development of targeted interventions and policies aimed at reducing suicide rates and addressing the mental health challenges faced by diverse populations worldwide.



Project Obstacles:

1.   Finding a Dataset: The task of acquiring a comprehensive dataset on suicide proves to be a formidable challenge, primarily due to the intricate web of ethical, legal and privacy consideration associated with handling sensitive mental health information.

 

2.  Lack of continental values: one notable challenge in this project is the dataset is mainly organized based on countries. This lack of continental columns poses a challenge in showing the severity of suicide by continent. To address this challenge, I enter continental values to the dataset based on countries by leveraging Wikipedia data for accuracy.

  

3.  Lack of fully qualified date column: The dataset contains only a Year column. To create a calendar table for slicing and dicing the data I create a Modified date column with a default date and month.

 

4.   Creating GIF: Since this was my first time creating GIF, it was difficult to be able to create the exact size. With a lot of practice and memorizing the details I can accomplish it.
